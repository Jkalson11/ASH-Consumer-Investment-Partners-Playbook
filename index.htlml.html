Investment Criteria

What We Look For

ASH invests in founder-led consumer businesses where our operating system, local Atlanta network, and capital can meaningfully accelerate value creation. We are highly selective and partner with operators we trust.

Sectors
Consumer Focus

Hospitality, specialty retail, consumer services, lifestyle, and consumer products. Businesses with a strong brand identity and passionate customer base.

Geography
Atlanta-Anchored

Primary focus on Atlanta and the Southeast. Businesses with a strong local presence and potential to expand within or beyond the region.

Stage
Growth to Mature

Established businesses with demonstrated product-market fit. We are not pre-revenue. We partner at inflection points where our operating system creates the most leverage.

Ownership
Majority & Minority

We invest in both majority and minority positions. In minority situations, we typically seek governance rights including board representation, information rights, and consent rights on major decisions.

Check Size
Flexible

Investment size is calibrated to the business's capital needs and value-creation opportunity. We are not constrained by fund mandate minimums or maximums — each partnership is structured appropriately.

What We Bring
Operators First

Capital is a commodity. We differentiate through our operating system, local network, sector expertise, and the ASH Playbook — a repeatable value-creation framework built on 20+ years of experience.

Ideal Partner Profile

  • Founder or operator with deep domain expertise and genuine passion for their business
  • Business with demonstrated customer loyalty, a defensible niche, and a clear path to margin expansion
  • Operator who wants a value-add partner — not just capital — and is open to performance management discipline
  • Willingness to run a shared operating cadence and transparent reporting culture
01 — Foundation

What ASH Is

ASH is an operator-led investment platform that partners with founder-operators to build enduring consumer businesses. We invest across majority and minority positions and add value through a disciplined operating system that improves execution, strengthens unit economics, and supports smart growth.

We operate across the consumer landscape:

  • Consumer Products — DTC, wholesale, omnichannel, marketplace
  • Consumer Services — membership, recurring revenue, multi-site, delivery-based
  • Hospitality — restaurants, hotels, clubs, entertainment-driven venues

While each sub-vertical has unique mechanics, the value-creation system is consistent: drive durable demand, improve unit economics, convert earnings to cash, and build scalable operating infrastructure.

We partner with management teams that share our unwavering commitment to the employee and customer experience as a means of growing the intrinsic value of their businesses and generating prosperity for all stakeholders.

03 — Thesis

Our Value-Creation Thesis

ASH creates value by installing the operating disciplines that high-performing consumer brands rely on—without stripping away the identity that made the business special.

We focus on the levers that reliably create enterprise value:

  • Revenue quality — demand generation, conversion, frequency/retention, mix, and pricing integrity
  • Margin expansion — COGS discipline, labor/delivery efficiency, waste control, and fixed-cost leverage
  • Cash generation — working capital discipline, forecasting rhythm, and capex planning
  • People and accountability — clear roles, coaching cadence, and performance management
  • Scalable infrastructure — reporting, SOPs, controls, and decision rights that scale beyond the founder
04 — Partnership Model

How We Work

ASH works best when the relationship is explicit. We define how we engage so operators get support—not noise.

What operators can expect from ASH
  • A clear operating cadence (weekly / monthly / quarterly)
  • A shared scorecard with definitions everyone agrees on
  • A 100-day plan anytime we invest or step into a new phase
  • Fast help on high-impact initiatives (pricing, labor models, purchasing, dashboards, SOPs, org design)
  • Decision support
What ASH expects from operators
  • A single accountable leader per function (Ops, Finance, Sales/Marketing, People)
  • Truthful reporting — good news and bad news, fast
  • Commitment to run the cadence for at least two full cycles (90 days) before rewriting the system
  • Discipline around fundamentals: cost controls, operating standards, and execution consistency

Ownership-Based Involvement

  • Majority — ASH leads governance and drives the operating cadence; deeper functional build-out; active talent support
  • Minority — ASH supports through scorecard design, monthly reviews, targeted projects, and operator enablement; influence through rigor, not control. In most cases, ASH still seeks governance rights (board seats, information rights, and consent rights on major decisions)
05 — Operating Motions

Value-Creation Operating Motions

ASH deploys one of two operating motions based on the business's starting point—ensuring our approach is stage-appropriate, measurable, and aligned to value creation.

Motion 1

Growth & Scale

For growth-stage brands: institutionalize the operating infrastructure required to scale without margin erosion or brand degradation. We focus on repeatable systems, leadership capacity, and durable unit economics.

Characteristics

  • Core return driver: revenue growth, market expansion
  • Primary value creation: scaling what already works
  • Upside: largely through revenue growth and slight margin improvement
  • Capital intensity: often requires follow-on capital

Primary Focus Areas

  • Scalable org design and leadership capacity
  • Throughput and capacity planning
  • Durable unit economics at scale
  • Operating cadence and management systems
  • Repeatability (SOPs, training, brand standards)
Motion 2

Performance Optimization

For established assets: improve predictability, profitability, and cash generation through execution rigor and structural efficiency. We prioritize near-term EBITDA improvement while building the cadence to sustain gains.

Characteristics

  • Core return driver: multiple expansion, margin expansion
  • Primary value creation: fixing underperformance
  • Upside: largely through key operational lever pulls
  • Capital intensity: often requires patient capital

Primary Focus Areas

  • Margin expansion (COGS, labor, waste, pricing/mix)
  • Cash and working capital discipline
  • Execution consistency and manager routines
  • Performance management system
  • Targeted growth where ROI is clear

Sub-Vertical Translation

  • Products — demand + channel economics + margin by channel + inventory turns
  • Services — utilization/capacity + CAC/LTV + labor delivery model + retention
  • Hospitality — throughput + labor scheduling discipline + mix/pricing + COGS and waste controls
06 — Financial Anchor

Financial North Star: Adjusted EBITDA

ASH anchors performance management on Adjusted EBITDA and the operational drivers that expand it: gross margin, labor/delivery efficiency, fixed-cost leverage, and disciplined discretionary spend.

Where appropriate, we track contribution margin by channel, site, or service line as a diagnostic layer to understand why EBITDA is moving.

Adjusted EBITDA Conventions

  • Establish definitions at the start — owner comp, one-time items, startup/expansion costs, extraordinary items
  • Maintain a consistent reconciliation so operators and investors read the same scorecard
  • Treat adjustments as transparency tools — not hiding places
07 — Diagnostic Framework

The 14-Day Diagnostic

At the start of each partnership—and at major inflection points—ASH runs a structured 14-day diagnostic to identify the primary constraint and route the engagement into the appropriate motion.

Outputs by Day 14

  • Motion selection (Scale vs Optimize) and sequencing
  • Top 5 priorities with owners, timelines, and KPI targets
  • Baseline scorecard with agreed definitions
  • Risk and dependency log (keyperson, vendor, cash, operational risk)

Diagnostic Lenses

A Financial Performance & EBITDA Quality
B Revenue Engine (Demand & Mix)
C Unit Economics
D Capacity, Throughput & Delivery
E Cash & Working Capital
F Org, Talent & Accountability
G Systems, Controls & Data Integrity

Routing Logic

→

If the constraint is capacity, infrastructure, leadership depth, or repeatability → route to Growth & Scale

→

If the constraint is economics leakage, execution inconsistency, or weak controls/cash conversion → route to Performance Optimization

→

If both are true → stabilize economics and controls first, then scale

08 — Operating Rhythm

The ASH Cadence

ASH is built around a simple principle: tight weekly execution + clear monthly accountability + quarterly strategic alignment.

Weekly

Operating Review

45 minutes

Agenda

  • Scorecard snapshot: revenue, gross margin, labor cost, EBITDA pacing
  • Variance drivers: what moved, why, what we'll do this week
  • Top constraints: 1–3 issues limiting performance
  • Initiative tracker: due items, blockers, resets
  • Decisions/escalations requiring partner input

Rules

  • No storytelling without numbers
  • Every issue ends with an owner + date
  • If it matters, it hits the tracker
Monthly

Performance Review

90 minutes

Agenda

  • Financial review: P&L, Adjusted EBITDA vs budget/forecast, bridge
  • Commercial review: revenue by channel/site, pricing, mix, retention
  • Cost review: COGS, labor model, fixed cost leverage, discretionary spend
  • Cash review: working capital, capex, liquidity outlook
  • Talent/org: capacity, key hires, performance issues
  • Next month priorities: confirm top 5, KPI targets, owners
Quarterly

Strategy Review

2–3 hours

Agenda

  • Progress vs thesis and roadmap
  • What to double down on / stop / redesign
  • Capital allocation and growth sequencing
  • Risk review and mitigation plan
  • Updated targets and initiatives for the next 90 days
09 — 100-Day Plan

Growth & Scale Motion

Objective: Scale the business by building operating infrastructure that enables growth without margin erosion, working-capital strain, or brand dilution.

01
Days 1–14

Baseline + Stabilize the Growth Engine

  • KPI baseline with clear definitions
  • EBITDA and margin fact base by channel/line/site
  • Constraint map: top 3 bottlenecks
  • Cadence launched with owners
02
Days 15–60

Build Capacity + Repeatability

  • Org and capability build plan
  • Repeatable execution system for core workflow
  • Forecasting rhythm: demand, labor, inventory, cash
  • Scale-safe playbook v1: SOPs that protect quality and margin
03
Days 61–100

Systemize + Prepare Next Growth Stage

  • Embedded management system with scorecard and variance routines
  • Reporting and close discipline
  • Growth roadmap (6–12 months) with budget gates and KPIs
  • Talent plan to reduce key-person dependency

Key KPIs

  • Adjusted EBITDA stability and pacing as volume grows
  • Gross margin and direct cost discipline (by channel/site)
  • Labor/delivery efficiency ratios and capacity utilization
  • Inventory turns / fill rate / returns (where relevant)
  • Forecast accuracy and cash conversion

Common Failure Modes

  • Scaling demand before capacity/systems are ready
  • Channel growth that is margin-negative
  • Inventory/capex getting ahead of cash conversion
  • Reporting without ownership and action
10 — 100-Day Plan

Performance Optimization Motion

Objective: Improve profitability, predictability, and cash generation through execution rigor and structural efficiency—without compromising the consumer experience.

01
Days 1–14

Baseline + Stop Leakage

  • KPI baseline with variance views; EBITDA bridge
  • Cash visibility: weekly liquidity + near-term obligations
  • Immediate controls installed (spend approvals, scheduling, discount governance)
  • Top leakage list with owners and weekly targets
02
Days 15–60

Structural Margin + Cash Improvements

  • Labor/delivery model reset (staffing standards, productivity targets)
  • COGS/vendor/procurement improvements and compliance
  • Pricing and discount governance
  • Working capital plan: inventory turns, payables discipline
03
Days 61–100

Sustain + Institutionalize

  • Embedded cadence: weekly execution + monthly performance review
  • SOPs/training for workflows that drive most outcomes
  • Org/talent adjustments where ownership is missing
  • 6–12 month roadmap with sequencing and KPI targets

Key KPIs

  • Adjusted EBITDA improvement vs baseline and plan
  • Gross margin and controllable expense ratios
  • Labor/delivery productivity ratios
  • Discount rate and promo ROI
  • Inventory turns / returns / shrink and cash conversion cycle

Common Failure Modes

  • Cutting costs that damage experience and demand
  • Too many initiatives running simultaneously
  • Avoiding accountability and necessary role changes
  • Fixing one channel while another silently bleeds
11 — Measurement

Scorecard Framework

One source of truth. EBITDA first, drivers beneath. Every KPI has an owner, and reds trigger actions with due dates.

Core Consumer Scorecard

01

Financial Performance

  • Revenue (MTD/QTD/YTD; vs Budget; vs Prior Year)
  • Gross margin % and $
  • Operating expenses % and $ (key categories)
  • Adjusted EBITDA $ and % (vs Budget / vs Prior Year)
  • EBITDA bridge (price/mix, volume, COGS, labor, fixed costs)
02

Unit Economics

  • Contribution margin by channel/site/line
  • Labor/delivery cost % + productivity ratio
  • COGS/direct costs % + key variance drivers
  • Discounting/promotions % and governance compliance
  • Returns/refunds/comp rate %
03

Growth & Commercial Health

  • Primary demand KPI (one per asset)
  • Conversion rate (if measurable)
  • Retention/repeat/churn (as applicable)
  • Mix shifts (channel/product/service/site)
  • Pricing integrity (net price realization)
04

Cash & Working Capital

  • Weekly cash + 13-week outlook
  • Inventory days/turns (if applicable)
  • AR/AP cadence and exceptions
  • Cash conversion cycle
  • Capex vs plan (timing + approvals)
05

Operating Execution & Risk

  • SLA / on-time / backlog (as applicable)
  • Quality failure rate (returns, complaints, rework)
  • Capacity utilization / throughput
  • Staffing health and key roles open
  • Top risks (vendor concentration, compliance, keyperson)

Add-On Driver Panels

Products

  • Revenue and margin by channel
  • CAC / ROAS / MER (where applicable)
  • Repeat rate, AOV (DTC), wholesale velocity/reorder
  • Returns rate and reasons
  • Fill rate / stockouts / inventory turns / forecast accuracy

Services

  • Leads/bookings → conversion; utilization rate
  • Retention/churn; ARPU
  • Labor efficiency (cost per job/appointment)
  • No-show/cancellation rate
  • Delivery quality / customer satisfaction

Hospitality

  • Demand KPI (covers/rooms/members) + mix
  • Check average / ADR where relevant
  • Labor % + productivity
  • COGS % + waste controls
  • Throughput constraints + guest sentiment trend

KPI Definitions to Lock at Start

  • Adjusted EBITDA (and reconciliation)
  • COGS/direct costs (in/out)
  • Labor/delivery costs (in/out)
  • Primary demand KPI
  • Primary quality KPI
