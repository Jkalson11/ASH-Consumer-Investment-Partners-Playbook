01 — Foundation

What ASH Is

ASH Consumer Investment Partners is an operator-led investment platform that partners with founder-operators to build enduring consumer businesses. We invest across majority and minority positions and add value through a disciplined operating system that improves execution, strengthens unit economics, and supports smart growth.

We operate across the consumer landscape, including:

  • Consumer Products — DTC, wholesale, omnichannel, marketplace
  • Consumer Services — membership, recurring revenue, multi-site, delivery-based
  • Hospitality — restaurants, hotels, clubs, entertainment-driven venues

While each sub-vertical has unique mechanics, the value-creation system is consistent: drive durable demand, improve unit economics, convert earnings to cash, and build scalable operating infrastructure. We partner with management teams that share our unwavering commitment to the employee and customer experience as a means of growing the intrinsic value of their businesses and generating prosperity for all stakeholders.

Our Vision

Employees

We employ extraordinary individuals who love working together. As a team we are greater than the sum of our parts, committed to supporting each other on and off the field so every person in our community is set up to live a full life and master their vocation.

Management Teams & Portfolio Companies

We are a sought-after partner for management teams looking to grow the intrinsic value of their businesses by placing the customer and employee experiences first. We help management teams identify and harness their secret sauce, build defensible moats, and maximize the value of their business.

Investors

The best capital allocators in the world want to invest with us because we generate top-decile returns built on high integrity, disciplined analysis, sector expertise, strategic and operational improvements in portfolio companies, and an unwavering commitment to the truth.

Service Providers

We feel understood and supported by our expert suppliers who share our core values — a high sense of urgency and standard of excellence. They are experts in their fields and apply practical expertise without blindly defaulting to theoretical norms.

03 — Investment Standards

Investment Requirements

Every potential investment must satisfy six threshold requirements before diligence proceeds. These criteria are not weighted or traded off against one another — a business that does not satisfy all six does not advance regardless of financial attractiveness. They reflect the specific type of business where the ASH operating system creates the most value and generates the most durable returns.

Criterion 01
Demonstrable Customer Loyalty

The business must have a proven, passionate customer base — patrons who return by choice, advocate to others, and would notice the absence of the brand. Operational excellence can improve a business that customers already love; it cannot manufacture the underlying affinity. Businesses without genuine customer loyalty are outside the ASH investment mandate.

Criterion 02
Meaningful Consumer Relevance

The business must play a meaningful role in how its customers live, eat, experience, or express themselves. We invest in businesses that matter to the communities they serve — not commodities, not purely transactional services. Relevance is a leading indicator of retention, pricing power, and long-term defensibility.

Criterion 03
Cultural Resonance & Longevity

The business must be woven into community and consumer lifestyle in a way that is enduring rather than trend-dependent. We invest in brands that have the potential to outlast cycles — businesses with a defined identity, a loyal constituency, and a reason to exist that does not depend on a single moment in culture.

Criterion 04
In-Person Experience Orientation

ASH has a strong preference for businesses centered on live, physical consumer experiences — restaurants, specialty retail, hospitality, events, and services. These businesses are inherently defensible against digital disruption, reward operational excellence disproportionately, and align with the core competencies of the ASH operating system.

Criterion 05
Return Threshold

The high-confidence investment case must support a 20% gross return as the minimum floor. The return pathway must be achievable through operational improvement and growth within the existing capital structure — not dependent on market conditions or outcomes outside ASH's control. Businesses requiring additional capital to survive do not meet this criterion; businesses requiring capital to accelerate growth beyond plan are evaluated on their merits.

Criterion 06
Operator Alignment

The founder or operating leadership must demonstrate openness to the ASH operating system — a shared cadence, transparent reporting, and accountability to agreed performance targets. The quality of the operator relationship is as important as the quality of the business. We invest in partnerships where both sides have made a deliberate choice to work together, with full understanding of what that entails.

04 — Operating Principles

The ASH Operating Commitments

The following commitments define how ASH conducts itself in every partnership — regardless of ownership structure, business stage, or deal complexity. They are not aspirational statements. They are behavioral standards that govern how we show up as investors, operators, and partners, and they are what operators and LPs should hold us accountable to.

Commitments to Portfolio Companies
  • We establish a shared scorecard with locked definitions before operational work begins — never after
  • We deliver a structured 100-day plan at every new investment and every major phase transition
  • We maintain the operating cadence without exception — consistent presence is a prerequisite for accountability
  • We communicate difficult findings directly and promptly; we do not soften assessments that require action
  • We protect the brand identity and consumer experience in every cost and operational decision
  • We bring institutional rigor without institutional overhead — the system scales to the business, not the reverse
  • We treat employee experience as a core operating lever, not a secondary consideration
  • We give operating partners full recognition for the results they drive
Operational Standards We Hold Ourselves To
  • We do not scale an operating model that has not been validated — volume amplifies both strengths and deficiencies
  • We do not rely on financial engineering as a primary return driver; EBITDA expansion through operations is the mandate
  • We do not invest in a business without demonstrated consumer affinity and assume that affinity can be created post-close
  • We do not pursue more concurrent initiatives than the operator can own with genuine accountability
  • We do not reduce costs in ways that compromise the consumer experience that underlies the investment thesis
  • We do not defer necessary accountability conversations — personnel, structure, or performance — because they are uncomfortable
  • We do not adjust scorecard definitions or performance targets in response to underperformance
  • We do not treat activity as a proxy for progress — every initiative requires a named owner and a committed date

06 — Value-Creation Thesis

08 — Operating Motions

The Investment Continuum

ASH operates across a continuum of investment situations, anchored at two ends by distinct operating motions. Every business enters the system somewhere along this continuum, and the specific playbook elements deployed are selected accordingly. The 14-Day Diagnostic determines both the appropriate motion and the discrete interventions that will generate the most value for that business. Deploying a uniform set of tools regardless of context is among the most common failure modes in PE operating partnerships.

Motion 1 — Growth-Oriented
Growth & Scale

Applied to businesses with demonstrated product-market fit that require operating infrastructure to scale without margin erosion, working-capital strain, or brand dilution. The mandate is to build the systems that make growth repeatable and defensible.

  • Core return driver: revenue growth, market expansion
  • Primary value creation: scaling what already works
  • Entry constraint: capacity, leadership depth, or repeatability
  • Capital profile: often requires follow-on capital to fund growth
Primary Focus Areas
  • Scalable org design, spans/layers, and leadership capacity
  • Throughput and capacity planning
  • Durable unit economics at scale
  • Operating cadence and management systems
  • Repeatability: SOPs, training systems, brand standards, QA
↔
Motion 2 — Operational Alpha
Operational Alpha

Applied to established businesses in strong verticals or markets where execution gaps, structural inefficiencies, or weak controls are suppressing value. The mandate is to improve profitability and predictability through rigorous operational intervention, entered at an attractive valuation.

  • Core return driver: margin expansion, multiple expansion, execution improvement
  • Primary value creation: closing the gap between current and potential performance
  • Entry constraint: economics leakage, inconsistent execution, or weak financial controls
  • Capital profile: typically patient capital; growth investment follows stabilization
Primary Focus Areas
  • Margin expansion: COGS discipline, labor model, waste, pricing/mix optimization
  • Cash and working capital management
  • Execution consistency and management routines
  • Performance management system installation
  • Targeted growth initiatives where ROI is clear and capital is committed

Selecting Playbook Elements

Within each motion, ASH identifies the specific playbook elements relevant to the individual business. A growth-stage hospitality brand requires different interventions than a mature specialty retailer experiencing margin compression. The Playbook is a toolkit; the 14-Day Diagnostic determines which tools to deploy and in what sequence.

Sub-Vertical Translation

  • Products — demand generation + channel economics + margin discipline by channel + inventory turns and working capital management
  • Services — capacity utilization + CAC/LTV ratio + labor delivery model + retention and churn management
  • Hospitality — throughput optimization + labor scheduling discipline + revenue mix and pricing + COGS and waste controls
09 — Financial North Star

Adjusted EBITDA

ASH anchors all performance management on Adjusted EBITDA and the operational drivers that expand it. This is the single number that governs weekly reviews, monthly accountability, and investment thesis tracking. Everything else is a driver of this number.

Driver 2
Labor%

Driver 3

Fixed-cost leverage — occupancy, G&A discipline, vendor contracts
Driver 4
Disc.
Discretionary spend governance — approvals, timing, ROI requirement

Adjusted EBITDA Conventions

  • Definitions are locked at the start of every engagement — owner comp treatment, one-time items, startup/expansion costs, extraordinary items
  • A consistent reconciliation is maintained so operators and ASH read the same number — no divergent scorecards
  • Adjustments are transparency tools, not hiding places. Every adjustment is documented and defensible
  • Contribution margin by channel, site, or service line is tracked as a diagnostic layer beneath EBITDA to explain movement
10 — Diagnostic Framework

The 14-Day Diagnostic

At the start of every partnership — and at every major inflection point — ASH runs a structured 14-day diagnostic to identify the primary constraint and route the engagement into the correct operating motion. Speed matters: a misdiagnosed motion wastes the first 60 days.

Outputs by Day 14

  • Motion selection (Growth & Scale vs Performance Optimization) with written rationale
  • Top 5 priorities with owners, timelines, and KPI targets
  • Baseline scorecard with agreed definitions — every KPI locked before work begins
  • Risk and dependency log: keyperson, vendor concentration, cash runway, operational single points of failure

Diagnostic Lenses

A · Financial
EBITDA Quality

P&L structure, margin by line, EBITDA bridge, adjustments audit, cash conversion

B · Commercial
Revenue Engine

Demand drivers, mix, pricing integrity, conversion rates, retention/churn

C · Economics
Unit Economics

Contribution margin by channel/site/line, CAC/LTV where applicable, payback periods

D · Operations
Capacity & Throughput

Constraint map, productivity ratios, quality failure rates, delivery reliability

E · Finance
Cash & Working Capital

Weekly cash position, 13-week outlook, inventory turns, AR/AP cadence

F · People
Org & Accountability

Role clarity, spans/layers, single points of failure, coaching cadence, key-person risk

G · Systems
Controls & Data Integrity

Reporting infrastructure, close process, approval workflows, POS/data accuracy, SOP coverage

Routing Logic

→

Constraint is capacity, infrastructure, leadership depth, or repeatability → Growth & Scale

→

Constraint is economics leakage, execution inconsistency, weak controls, or cash conversion → Performance Optimization

→

Both are true → Stabilize economics and controls first, then scale. Never scale a broken model.

13 — 100-Day Plan

Growth & Scale Motion

Objective: Build the operating infrastructure that enables growth without margin erosion, working-capital strain, or brand dilution. Scale the engine — don't just push more through a broken one.

  • Demand clarity: what is driving growth and is it durable?
Days 15–60
02
Build Capacity + Repeatability
  • Org and capability build plan: roles, spans/layers, hiring sequence
  • Repeatable execution system for the core workflow
  • Forecasting rhythm: demand, labor/capacity, inventory, cash

03

  • Building reporting without assigning ownership — data without accountability

18 — Reference

Glossary of Terms

All definitions are locked at the start of each engagement. Any deviation from these definitions must be documented and agreed upon in writing. Inconsistent definitions are one of the most common sources of scorecard disputes.

Adjusted EBITDA
Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, further adjusted for owner compensation above/below market rate, non-recurring items, and startup/expansion costs. Adjustments must be documented and consistent period-to-period.
Contribution Margin
Revenue minus variable costs (COGS + variable labor + direct delivery costs) for a specific channel, site, or service line. Used as a diagnostic layer beneath EBITDA to identify where the business makes and loses money.
COGS / Direct Costs
All costs directly attributable to producing the product or delivering the service: food & beverage cost, product cost, direct materials, packaging. Does not include labor unless explicitly defined as direct labor. Definition locked at engagement start.
Labor / Delivery Cost
All hourly and salaried wages plus benefits and payroll taxes for employees directly involved in operations. Management-level employees are included unless separately classified. Definition of "in vs out" locked at engagement start.
EBITDA Bridge
A structured variance decomposition that explains the movement in Adjusted EBITDA from one period to another (or vs budget) by attributing the change to specific drivers: price/mix, volume, COGS, labor, fixed costs, and other.
Primary Demand KPI
The single leading indicator of revenue for a given business. Examples: covers (restaurant), bookings (services), units sold (products), members (clubs). One KPI per asset, defined and locked at engagement start.
Attach Rate
The percentage of transactions or covers that include a specific add-on category (e.g., wine, dessert, starter). Calculated as: transactions with the add-on ÷ total eligible transactions. Used in hospitality to measure upsell execution.
CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
Total sales and marketing spend in a period divided by the number of new customers acquired in that period. Used in services and DTC products. Must define what counts as a "new customer" at engagement start.
LTV (Lifetime Value)
The projected total revenue or contribution margin from a single customer over their relationship with the business. Calculated as: average order value × purchase frequency × average customer lifespan.
13-Week Cash Forecast
A rolling weekly cash flow projection covering 13 weeks forward, showing opening cash, inflows, outflows, and closing cash by week. Updated weekly. Primary tool for cash and liquidity management.
Working Capital
Current assets minus current liabilities. In the context of the ASH operating system, tracked primarily through inventory days, accounts receivable days, and accounts payable days (the cash conversion cycle).
Comp Rate
The percentage of total revenue that is comped (provided at no charge or at a discount for service recovery, marketing, or employee meals). Target: below 1% of total revenue. All comp types tracked separately.
Operating Motion
The designated strategic mode for a given engagement: either Growth & Scale (building infrastructure to grow) or Performance Optimization (improving profitability of an existing base). Selected via the 14-Day Diagnostic and documented in the 100-Day Plan.
Initiative Tracker
The shared document tracking all active improvement initiatives across the business. Each item includes: initiative description, owner (single person), due date, current status, and any blockers. Reviewed at every weekly operating meeting.
Keyperson Risk
The operational or relational dependency on a single individual whose departure would materially impair business performance. Assessed in the 14-Day Diagnostic and tracked in the risk log. Mitigation is a required element of every 100-Day Plan.
